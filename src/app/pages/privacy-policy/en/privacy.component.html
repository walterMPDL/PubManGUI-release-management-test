Data protection information

At a glance

The Max Planck Digital Library (MPDL) takes the protection of your personal data very seriously. In doing so, we comply with the relevant provisions of data protection laws, in particular the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and the German Federal Data Protection Act (BDSG).

This document is intended to provide you with detailed information about how we use the data collected during your visit to our website and to inform you of your options and your rights under data protection law.

Description and scope of data processing

Provision of the website and log files

Every time you visit our website, our system, i.e. the web server, automatically collects information from the system of your calling computer or end device.

We collect the following data:

  • • Information about the browser type and version used
  • • The operating system of the end device
  • • The internet service provider
  • • The IP address
  • • The date and time of access
  • • The previous website from which the user accessed our website (referrer URL)
Purpose of data processing

Our system needs to store your IP address temporarily to enable delivery of the website to your device. To do this, the IP address of the end device must remain stored for the duration of the session. The above data is stored in so-called log files to ensure the functionality of our website. In addition, this data helps us to optimize the website and to ensure the security of our information technology systems (e.g. to detect attacks).

Legal basis

The legal basis for the temporary storage of this data and the log files is Art. 6 Para. 1 Subpara. 1 lit. f GDPR (our legitimate interests as the website operator in the secure, smooth and legally compliant provision of the website).

Storage duration

The above data is deleted as soon as it is no longer required for the purpose for which it was collected. In the case of data collection for the provision of the website, this is the case when the respective session has ended.

If the data is stored in log files, this is the case after seven days at the latest. It is possible to store the data for a longer period. In this case, we will delete or anonymize the user's IP address so that it can no longer be assigned to the accessing client and the data contained can no longer be linked to a particular person.

User accounts

Setting up a user account involves the storage and processing of personal data, namely surname, first name, institute affiliation and e-mail address. The data is used exclusively for the purpose of using the services of MPG.PuRe.

Employees of the Max Planck Society who are responsible for maintaining the data at their institutes or facilities can request the creation of such an account from the Max Planck Digital Library (MPDL) by sending an e-mail to pure-support@listsrv.mpg.de.

Purpose of processing

The creation of a user account is mandatory for entering and managing publication data in MPG.PuRe. It is not possible to use these functionalities without creating an account and the associated storage of personal data.

Legal basis

The data is stored and processed on the basis of legitimate interest in accordance with Art. 6 (1) f) GDPR. Our legitimate interest arises from the above-mentioned purpose for data collection.

Storage duration

The user accounts and the associated personal data will be deleted at your request within one month at the latest.

Processing many authors in the edit mask using AI

In the edit mask, it is possible to enter many authors in one field using copy&paste (e.g. from the respective PDF or from a website). This data is then separated by name and first name using artificial intelligence and entered into the input mask in a structured manner.

For this purpose, the entered data is automatically forwarded via an interface to the chat service “Chat AI” of the AI Service Center for Sensitive and Critical Infrastructures (KISSKI) (https://kisski.gwdg.de/) of the Gesellschaft für wissenschaftliche Datenverarbeitung mbH Göttingen (GWDG).

The Large Language Model (LLM) “Llama” is used, which is hosted by the GWDG and therefore no contents of the requests (chat histories) are stored.

Purpose of data processing

The ability to insert long author lists from unstructured data into the input mask is an optional service. It makes work considerably easier, as this data can otherwise only be entered manually.

Legal basis

The data is processed on the basis of legitimate interest in accordance with Art. 6 para. 1 f) GDPR. Our legitimate interest arises from the above-mentioned purpose for data collection.

Duration of storage

KISSKI does not store any content from the requests (chat histories) on its servers

Session cookies

We use so-called “session cookies” to make it easier for you to use our website. These are small text files that are only stored on your hard drive for the duration of your visit to our website and, depending on the settings of your browser program, are deleted again when you close your browser. These cookies do not retrieve any information about you that is stored on your hard drive and do not affect your files.

Most browsers are set to accept cookies automatically. However, you can disable the storage of cookies or set your browser to alert you when cookies are sent. Please note that disabling cookies may affect some of the features on our website.

Website analysis

We use the open-source web analysis service Matomo (formerly Piwik).

Purpose of processing

We use Matomo to analyze the use of our website and to make regular improvements. The statistics obtained help us to optimize our services and make them more interesting for you as a user.

Every time you visit our website, our system collects the following data and information from the computer system of the visiting computer:

  • • IP address, anonymized by shortening it
  • • Two cookies to distinguish between different visitors (pk_id and pk_sess)
  • • Previously visited URL (referrer), if transmitted by the browser
  • • Name and version of the operating system
  • • Name, version and language setting of the browser

Additionally, if JavaScript is activated:

  • • Visited URLs on this website
  • • Time of page views
  • • Type of HTML requests
  • • screen resolution and color depth
  • • Vom Browser unterstützte Techniken und Formate (z. B. Cookies, Java, Flash, PDF)

The data is stored and analyzed exclusively on a central server operated by the Max Planck Society.

Only anonymized data is stored and used with Matomo technology. The IP addresses are anonymized immediately after processing and before they are stored. They do not allow any identification of visitors to this website. Your data will not be passed on to third parties. Matomo uses cookies. The information generated by the cookie in the pseudonymous user profile is not used to personally identify the visitor to a website and is not merged with personal data about the pseudonym holder.

Legal basis

The legal basis for the use of Matomo is Art. 6 Para. 1 Subpara. 1 lit. a GDPR (your consent, which you have given via our consent banner). You can revoke this consent at any time with effect for the future.

Storage duration

The data is usually stored for 30 days and deleted after the final annual totals for the access statistics have been formed.

Revocation of consent

Of course, you have the option to revoke your consent to this data collection with effect for the future. Data already legally collected up to the point of consent revocation is not affected. You have the following independent options to prevent data collection by the central server:

  • • Activate the “Do-Not-Track” or “Do Not Follow” setting in your browser. As long as this setting is active, our central Matomo server will not store any of your data. Important: The Do-Not-Track instruction usually only applies to the one device and the one browser in which you activate the setting. If you use multiple devices/browsers, you must activate Do-Not-Track separately everywhere.
  • • Use our opt-out function. Click on the tick in the following selection box to stop or reactivate data collection. As long as the selection box is deactivated, our central Matomo server will not store any of your data. Important: For the opt-out, we have to store a special recognition cookie in your browser. If you delete this or use a different PC/browser, you will have to object to data collection again on this page.

The storage and analysis of data is always done anonymously. Session cookies are deleted at the latest when you close your browser. Personal user profiles are not created.

Your rights

According to the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), you have the right:

  • • to request information about your personal data processed by us in accordance with Art. 15 GDPR. This includes the purposes of processing, the categories of personal data, the categories of recipients of the data, the planned storage period, the origin of your data, and the existence of automated decision-making including profiling;
  • • in accordance with Art. 16 GDPR, to request the correction of incorrect or incomplete personal data stored by us;
  • • in accordance with Art. 17 GDPR, to request the deletion of your personal data stored by us, unless the processing is necessary to exercise the right to freedom of expression and information, to fulfill a legal obligation, for reasons of public interest or to assert, exercise or defend legal claims;
  • • in accordance with Art. 18 GDPR, to demand the restriction of the processing of your personal data if you dispute the accuracy of the data, if the processing is unlawful and you refuse the deletion of your data, and in cases where we no longer need your data but you need it to enforce legal claims. A restriction of processing will also be carried out if you have objected to the processing but it is not yet certain whether our legitimate interests outweigh yours;
  • • in accordance with Art. 20 GDPR, to receive the personal data concerning you, which you have provided to us, in a structured, commonly used and machine-readable format and have the right to transmit those data to another controller;
  • • in accordance with Art. 7 (3) GDPR, to withdraw consent at any time. The data processed before we receive your request may still be legally processed;
  • • PURSUANT TO ART. 21 GDPR, YOU HAVE THE RIGHT TO OBJECT TO THE PROCESSING OF YOUR PERSONAL DATA. IF THE PROCESSING OF YOUR DATA IS BASED ON THE LEGITIMATE INTEREST OF THE MPDL OR THIRD PARTIES AND YOUR OBJECTION IS BASED ON YOUR PARTICULAR SITUATION, WE WILL COME ACCORDINGLY COMPLY WITH THAT REQUEST, UNLESS THERE ARE GROUNDS FOR PROCESSING THAT OUTWEIGH YOUR INTERESTS THAT ARE WORTHY OF PROTECTION, OR WE NEED YOUR DATA TO ENFORCE LEGAL CLAIMS.
  • • Complain to a supervisory authority in accordance with Art. 77 GDPR. If you believe that we have not sufficiently complied with your rights and our obligations under the General Data Protection Regulation, you have the right to file a complaint with a data protection authority. The authority responsible for us is the Bavarian State Office for Data Protection Supervision (BayLDA), Promenade 18, 91522 Ansbach.
